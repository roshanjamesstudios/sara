Side A
The Origin Tracks
01
Beastie Boys & Gretsch Dreams
feat. The Touring Days · Pop Nation · Midtown Radio

Before the cameras, before all of the titles — there was a girl with a guitar and a voice that could fill any room in Kitchener. Sara fronted a touring band, wrote songs, shredded on her mint Gretsch, and lived the raw, beautiful chaos of being a working musician. She's still that girl — she sings with Pop Nation, hosts on Midtown Radio, and the Beastie Boys hoodie isn't nostalgia. It's identity.

02
Playtime: The Movie
feat. Princess Cinemas · Waterloo Arts Fund

Sara made a whole film. And it premiered at Princess Cinemas — KW's legendary art house — with her name right up on that marquee next to Banshees of Inisherin and Tár. November 2022. A Film by Sara Geidlinger, up in lights.

03
This Mixtape Is For You
feat. The Registry Theatre · Kitchener · January 2024

The concert that named this whole tribute. "This Mixtape Is For You" at the Registry Theatre — a live show that brought together a whole community of musicians, artists, and beautiful humans. Sara was right there in the middle of it all - performing as a soloist with her voice and guitar, her heart, and her unshakeable ability to make everyone feel like they belong on that stage.

04
Ever-Changing World (The PR Cut)
feat. August Eleven · Patrick LaFrenere · 2025

Sara creates high-impact, successful launch campaigns for creative projects — and the rollout for Patrick's "Ever-Changing World" single under his August Eleven artist name was no exception. She brought strategic PR vision, media outreach, and her signature ability to make something land exactly when and how it needs to. She's the person you hire when you're putting something real into the world.

05
Perimeter (The Physics Board)
feat. Perimeter Institute · Waterloo

Sara does creative work for the Perimeter Institute — where theoretical physicists scrawl equations that try to explain the actual fabric of reality. And Sara just fits right in. Thumbs up in front of the blackboard, totally at home among the Feynman diagrams. Because that's Sara: equally comfortable with a guitar, a camera, or a quantum field theory chalkboard.

Side B
The Deep Cuts
06
Bonn Park Media Inc.
feat. Hip Hop Hope · Lullaby Project · Grand River Film Fest · WLU

Sara didn't wait for permission to build a media company. She just did it. Bonn Park Media is the vessel for her documentaries, podcasts, and socially conscious storytelling — including the Lullaby Project and "Hip Hop Hope: Underground Rhythms and Anti-Racism in Waterloo Region," an Official Selection at the Windsor International Black Film Festival. She's been featured at the Grand River Film Festival, earned her Master of Community Music from Wilfrid Laurier University, and runs the whole operation with the kind of creative hustle that would make any 90s indie label founder proud.

07
The Creative Table
feat. Retreats · Spring Rolls · Chess · Community

Whether it's a creative retreat with women laughing around a farmhouse table, making spring rolls in a kitchen full of love, playing chess on the porch with the dog sleeping nearby, or rallying a whole community around a shared project — Sara builds tables where people can sit together and make things. That's her superpower.

08
Dog Mom Energy
feat. Puppy Kisses · Glow Stick Walks · Golf Course Hangs

The puppy face-licking photo. The glow stick dog walks at dusk. Three dogs sitting at attention on a golf course while Sara stands there like the queen she is. This woman doesn't just love her dogs — she builds a whole lifestyle around them.

09
West Coast Transmission
feat. Colin Hunter · Royal Roads · BC Forests · Beach Crabs

Sara and Colin are heading west. He's working on Poly Crisis research at Royal Roads University while they continue making films together. This isn't an ending — it's a frequency shift. The same Sara energy, beamed from Pacific beaches full of driftwood and crabs, mossy forests, and that endless BC coastline.

Liner Notes
From the Label

Sara — this mixtape is for you.

You're the person who shows up with a camera in one hand and a guitar in the other. Who builds a media company and still makes time for spring rolls. Who puts a film up on the marquee at Princess Cinemas and creates high-impact PR campaigns for your friends' music releases because that's just what you do.

You've been nominated for Woman of the Year, but honestly, you're Woman of Every Year. You make documentaries about things that matter — Hip Hop Hope was an Official Selection at the Windsor International Black Film Festival. You give a thumbs up in front of a physics blackboard at the Perimeter Institute like it's the most natural thing in the world.

You earned your Master of Community Music from Wilfrid Laurier. You toured in a band. You sing with Pop Nation. You host on Midtown Radio. You built Bonn Park Media from scratch. You raised amazing kids. You loved the hell out of every dog you've ever met. And now you're taking your magic to the West Coast with Colin, where the forests are taller and the beaches are wild and the crabs don't know how lucky they are.

Forest Gold Records may not have fully launched yet — but this right here? This is our debut release. Catalogue number FGR-001. And it's all you.

- Roshan
